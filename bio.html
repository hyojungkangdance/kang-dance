bio

Hyo-Jung Kang is a South Korean-born Principal Dancer of the Semperoper Ballett Dresden, a position she has held since August 2025. From 2021 until August 2025 she was Principal Dancer at the Wiener Staatsballett, where she expanded her repertoire and became known for her dramatic sophistication and refined classical artistry.

She received her early training at Sunhwa Arts School and the Kirov Academy of Ballet in Washington, D.C., before completing her studies at the prestigious John Cranko School in Stuttgart. Upon graduation in 2003, she joined Stuttgart Ballet, rising through the ranks to become Principal Dancer in 2011.

Celebrated for her musicality, expressive intelligence and sculptural movement quality, she has performed many of the major roles in the repertoire, including Tatiana and Olga (Onegin), Juliet (Romeo and Juliet), Odette/Odile (Swan Lake), Giselle, Aurora (The Sleeping Beauty), Kitri (Don Quixote) and Manon Lescaut (The Lady of the Camellias). Her versatility spans a wide range of neoclassical and contemporary works by George Balanchine, Jerome Robbins, Hans van Manen, John Neumeier, Kenneth MacMillan, Jiří Kylián, William Forsythe and other leading choreographers.

A laureate of the Prix de Lausanne (2002), Hyo-Jung Kang has been repeatedly nominated as “Dancer of the Year” by tanz magazine. Alongside her performing career, she is active as a teacher, coach and mentor in international training programmes and cultural education initiatives.
